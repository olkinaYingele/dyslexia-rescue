Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 24, 2026

Read Me Aloud is an assistive reading app designed to help children with dyslexia read printed or photographed text. This policy explains what information the app processes, how it is used, and your rights as a user.

Zero-Data-Collection Commitment: We believe in absolute privacy for children. Our app is designed so that we, the developers, do not collect, store, or have access to any of your personal data, photos, scanned text, or voiceovers. Everything stays under your control.

1. Information We Collect and Process

The app does not require a user account and does not ask for or collect your name, email address, phone number, physical location, persistent device identifiers, or contact details.

  • Photos and Images — When you photograph a document or select an image from your gallery, that image is processed temporarily and transmitted to Google's Gemini API solely to perform optical character recognition (OCR) and extract the text visible in the image. While the app itself does not collect personal data, images selected or photographed by the user (such as school worksheets or book pages) may contain personal information (such as names, school details, or handwriting) visible within the image. This data is processed in transit and is not permanently stored on any external server operated by us.

2. Third-Party Services and AI Safety

To provide OCR and text-to-speech capabilities, the app securely routes data to the following external services:

  • Google Gemini API (iOS and Android) — Images are transmitted securely via HTTPS to Google Gemini API endpoints for text extraction.
    AI Data Safety & Retention: This app integrates with Google's API under developer/paid service terms. According to Google's official Gemini API developer terms, data submitted through paid developer API services is not used to train Google's public AI models. However, to maintain the safety and security of the service and monitor for potential misuse or abuse, Google may temporarily retain prompts, context, and outputs for up to fifty-five (55) days in accordance with its developer terms: ai.google.dev/gemini-api/terms.
  • Google Cloud Text-to-Speech API (Android only) — On Android devices, the extracted text is sent to Google's servers to generate the audio voiceover. On iOS devices, text-to-speech is performed locally on the device using Apple's built-in AVSpeechSynthesis engine; no text or audio data is transmitted externally on iOS for this purpose.
  • Network Transit Security — Because the app communicates directly with Google's cloud APIs, your device's IP address is naturally visible to Google during secure HTTPS request routing. This is a standard technical requirement of internet protocols. We do not store, log, or have access to your IP address.

3. Data Storage, Retention, and Deletion Policy

Scanned images and their extracted text are stored strictly on your local device inside the app's local sandbox storage. This allows the user to access and replay recent scans. We do not operate any server-side database or cloud infrastructure to store your scanned images, extracted text, or audio.

The app currently keeps up to 30 recent scans on the device. Each saved scan may include the original image and the text extracted from it. When the storage limit is reached and a new scan is added, the app uses a first-in, first-out (FIFO) retention rule: the oldest saved scan and its associated data are automatically and permanently deleted from the device's local memory.

Users can also delete any saved scan manually at any time using the delete button shown for that scan. All locally stored scans and extracted text can also be removed instantly by clearing the app's storage/cache or by uninstalling the app from the device.

4. Device Permissions

  • Camera — Used only when you explicitly tap to photograph printed text for reading. The camera stream is local, and photos are processed as described in Section 2.
  • Photo Library / Media — Used only when you choose to import an existing photo of a text document from your gallery.

The app does not request access to the device microphone, precise location, or device contacts.

5. Children's Privacy (COPPA & GDPR Compliance)

This app is designed as an accessibility tool to help children with dyslexia read printed or photographed text. To protect children's privacy, we encourage the app to be used under the guidance of a parent, guardian, or educator.

By design, the app complies with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) regarding children's data. The app does not ask children to provide personal information such as name, email address, phone number, or location. We do not track user behavior, we do not use advertising IDs, and we do not collect usage analytics. The only content processed is the image the user chooses to scan, which is processed transitively as described above to provide immediate reading assistance.

6. Data Security

We are committed to securing your data. All communication between the app and third-party APIs, such as Google Gemini and Google Cloud Text-to-Speech, occurs exclusively over encrypted HTTPS connections. We do not sell, rent, or share user data with third parties for advertising, marketing, or tracking purposes.

7. Analytics and Advertising

To maintain a distraction-free and private environment, the app does not contain any third-party analytics SDKs, tracking pixels, crash-reporting trackers, or advertising networks.

8. Your Rights

Since we do not maintain user accounts or operate server-side databases, we cannot identify, export, correct, or delete user-specific data on our own infrastructure. Saved scans are stored only on the user's device. Users can delete individual saved scans inside the app, clear the app's storage/cache, or uninstall the app to remove all locally stored scans and extracted text.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time to comply with new regulations or reflect updates in the app's functionality. The "Last updated" date at the top will indicate when changes were made. We encourage parents and guardians to review this policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect children's privacy.
